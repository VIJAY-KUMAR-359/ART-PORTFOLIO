Divine Duality
The Spiritual Duality—One Form, Two Souls. ---> A masterpiece by EPPILI VIJAY KUMAR ---> Medium: Black Sketch Pen and Oil Pastels on Paper ---> This artwork is a powerful representation of Lord Shiva and Lord Ganesha, merging two divine forms into a single expressive face. Created using a combination of black sketch pen for detailed line work and oil pastels for depth and shading, the piece captures a sense of realism and spiritual symbolism. On the left, the silhouette of Lord Shiva stands boldly with his trident, emerging from the form of Lord Ganesha—his son—whose expressive eye and features dominate the right. The interplay of light and shadow symbolizes the divine balance of destruction and creation, power and wisdom, inviting viewers to pause and reflect on the unity of opposites in the spiritual realm.
Little Krishna
Divine Innocence – Little Krishna..........Medium: Oil Pastels & Markers ..........This artwork is a heartfelt representation of Lord Krishna as a child, blending realism with vibrant color to capture his playful charm and divine innocence. Created using oil pastels and fine markers, the piece brings out expressive details in Krishna’s eyes, his peacock feather crown, and traditional adornments — all set against a natural outdoor backdrop that enhances the spiritual essence of the piece. The inspiration behind this creation came from a poll I conducted on my Instagram story, where followers were invited to choose between “Cute Little Krishna” and “Lord Shiva” as the next subject. With overwhelming love and support, Little Krishna emerged as the favorite — making this artwork a result of shared sentiment and divine direction.
Ardhanari Krishna-Radha
This vibrant and symbolic watercolor painting portrays a fusion of Lord Krishna and Radha as a single divine entity, inspired by the concept of Ardhanarishvara — the union of masculine and feminine energies. The left side of the image is Krishna, marked by the blue skin tone, peacock feather, and flute-like adornments, symbolizing divine love, cosmic harmony, and spiritual joy. The right side is Radha, adorned with elegant jewelry, a vibrant pink veil (ghoonghat), and expressive eyes that mirror grace, devotion, and earthly love. Together, this artwork represents unity, balance, and the inseparability of love and divinity, showing how Radha and Krishna are two halves of one eternal soul. The split down the middle emphasizes duality and oneness — a powerful reminder of the sacred balance in the universe. "Two souls, one heart in sacred grace, Krishna and Radha share one face. Divine in love, in hues they blend, A timeless bond that knows no end. "
